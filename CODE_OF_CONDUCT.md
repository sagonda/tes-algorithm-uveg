# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## 🎯 Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community founded on **respect, scientific integrity, and collaboration**.

---

## 📋 Our Standards

### Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- 🤝 Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
- 🎯 Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- 💬 Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- 🏆 Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes
- 🧠 Focusing on what is best for the community beyond the individual
- 📚 Acknowledging and respecting the intellectual property rights of others
- 🔬 Maintaining scientific rigor and honesty in code and documentation
- 👥 Supporting the professional development of community members

### Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- 😔 The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
- 🚫 Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- ⚖️ Public or private harassment
- 📧 Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address, without their explicit permission
- 🔓 Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting
- 💰 Sexual or commercial coercion
- 🤥 Plagiarism or intellectual property theft
- 📢 Discriminatory language based on protected attributes
- 🎯 Retaliation against individuals for reporting violations

---

## 🔍 Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to:

- Remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct
- Temporarily or permanently ban any contributor whose behavior is inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful
- Document and communicate enforcement actions to affected parties

---

## 📢 Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples of representing our community include using an official e-mail address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event.

**Community spaces include:**
- GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions)
- GitHub wikis and documentation
- Community email lists
- Project repositories and branches
- Conference presentations and workshops
- Any communication channel associated with the TES Algorithm UVEG project

**Report Contents Should Include:**
- Description of the incident
- Names/usernames of involved parties (if known)
- Screenshots or direct links to violations
- Context of the situation
- Your contact information
- Any additional evidence

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

### Response Process

1. **Initial Response:** Acknowledgment within 48 business hours
2. **Investigation:** Assessment of the reported behavior (3-5 business days)
3. **Decision:** Determination of violation and appropriate action
4. **Communication:** Notification to both parties of outcome
5. **Follow-up:** Monitoring to ensure compliance and prevent recurrence

### Confidentiality

All reports will be handled with confidentiality. The identity of the reporter will be protected except where necessary for investigation or legal requirements.

## 🎯 Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action deemed in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence:** A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

**Monitoring:** Continued monitoring for repeated behavior.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact:** A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence:** A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media.

**Duration:** Typically 30 days, may be extended.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact:** A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence:** A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period.

**Duration:** Typically 90 days to 6 months.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact:** Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence:** A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the project community.

**Appeal Process:** May appeal after 12 months with evidence of rehabilitation.

## 🌍 Specific Guidelines for Scientific/Technical Community

### Academic Integrity

- ✅ Properly attribute all sources and prior work
- ✅ Provide accurate descriptions of algorithms and methods
- ✅ Disclose limitations and assumptions in implementations
- ✅ Report bugs and issues honestly and transparently

### Professional Conduct

- ✅ Engage in constructive technical discussions
- ✅ Accept evidence-based feedback
- ✅ Maintain professional tone in all communications
- ✅ Respect the time and expertise of reviewers and maintainers

### Inclusive Technical Community

- ✅ Welcome contributors of all experience levels
- ✅ Provide patient, clear explanations
- ✅ Acknowledge good questions and contributions regardless of source
- ✅ Support diversity in technical perspectives and approaches
